Academic advising that protects the students CUNY forgets.

Project DJA is a compliance-aware AI advisor built for CUNY's 225,000 students — a population that is majority first-generation, working, and non-traditional. It reads a student's degree, career goal, visa status, financial aid, and work schedule, then produces a semester plan that protects each one before suggesting a single course.

▲ Why the stakes are different at CUNY

For a typical student, an advisor's mistake is an inconvenience. For a CUNY student, it can be a crisis. An international student who drops below 12 credits risks deportation. A TAP recipient who takes the wrong elective loses their funding. We don't just suggest classes — we provide compliance-aware guidance.

§ 01 / THE PROBLEM

Human advisors optimize for retention. DegreeWorks enforces the degree. Neither protects the student.

Most CUNY students get roughly thirty minutes with an overworked advisor once a semester. That advisor is trained to keep students enrolled — not to weigh their career goals against their visa rules, their TAP eligibility, their Pell proration, and their 40-hour week at the same time. DegreeWorks shows what's required for the degree, but it doesn't know the student is on an F-1 visa, works full-time, or is trying to pivot into robotics.

The result is predictable and documented: students who take "one easy elective" that quietly breaks their TAP eligibility. International students who register for a second online class and unknowingly violate their I-20. Working adults who accidentally take a 10 a.m. section that forces them to quit their job. Every one of these mistakes is preventable with deterministic rules and five minutes of the right questions.

§ 02 / THE COMPLIANCE GUARDRAIL

Four rules that run before the AI speaks.

F1
Immigration Status

International (F-1 / J-1)

Must maintain 12+ credits per term as an undergraduate. Only one online course counts toward the full-time requirement.

Risk of failure · Visa revocation
$T
New York State Aid

TAP Recipients

Must maintain 12+ credits that specifically satisfy degree requirements. Electives outside the allowable list are disqualified.

Risk of failure · Loss of funding
$P
Federal Aid

Pell Grant

Award is prorated by enrollment: 12 credits = 100%, 9 = 75%, 6 = 50%. Silent reductions become surprise tuition balances.

Risk of failure · Unexpected tuition balance
W
Life Constraints

Working Adults

Filter for evening, weekend, or asynchronous sections based on the hours the student is unavailable. No time-sinks.

Risk of failure · Academic burnout

§ 05 / PUBLIC INTEREST TECHNOLOGY

All nine guidelines, by design.

i.
Systems Thinking

We model the full system: degree, visa, aid, schedule, career. A "good" course that breaks any layer isn't good.

ii.
Ethical Decision-Making

The AI never claims authority. Every output cites a requirement and directs the student to confirm with their advisor.

iii.
Equity & Inclusion

The guardrail exists because advising failures fall hardest on international, low-income, and working students.

iv.
Engage Communities

Our team of three CUNY students is the user. Every requirement was written from lived experience.

v.
Interdisciplinarity

We combine education policy, immigration law, financial aid rules, and software engineering in one tool.

vi.
Transparency & Accountability

Each recommendation shows the requirement it satisfies, the compliance checks it passed, and plain-language reasoning.

vii.
Continuous Learning

Catalog and guardrail rules are modular data, not baked-in model weights. Update policy, not the AI.

viii.
PIT Technological Intuition

We deliberately chose not to use AI for compliance. Visa and aid rules must be deterministic, not probabilistic.

ix.
Champion Public Interest

CUNY's 225,000 students deserve guidance that protects their legal status, funding, and time — not just their credit count.
